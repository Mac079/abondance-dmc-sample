Our approach

Corporate events became
industrialised.
We built Abondance to fix that.

The same hotel ballroom. The same three dinner venues. The same team-building formats your guests have sat through in four other cities. Eugénie O'Byrne Peyre spent years inside luxury marketing and strategy consulting watching that happen. Watching people leave events having felt nothing.

Abondance was founded on a different premise: that an event should feel like a moment in someone's life, not a line item in a budget. We design every experience from your organisation's identity outward: who you are, what you need your guests to feel, and what Paris can do that nowhere else can.

Working with us

One brief.
One point of contact.
No standard formats.

We are a deliberately small agency. Every project is handled by Eugénie and a senior team member, not handed to a junior account manager once the contract is signed. You have a single point of contact from first conversation to the morning after the event.

We work with international agencies, executive assistants to C-suite, and incentive travel buyers planning events for between 10 and 400 guests. We speak English, French, and the language of people who have done this long enough to know when something is going to be remarkable.

a.

Brief

Tell us your objectives, your guests, your dates, and your instincts. We handle the rest.

b.

Concept

We design a bespoke proposal (venues, experiences, narrative arc) built around your organisation specifically.

c.

Execution

We manage every supplier, every timeline, every logistical detail. You arrive on the day. We make it happen.

d.

After

A full debrief, guest feedback, and documentation. Because the event should be a reference point, not a one-off.
